&#x20;**Case Study – Cyberbullying**



Cyberbullying is a type of cybercrime where a person is repeatedly harassed, threatened, or embarrassed online through social media, messages, or other digital platforms. It often happens among students and young people who are very active online.



A realistic example can be seen in college environments. A student shares a normal photo or opinion on social media. A few classmates start making fun of it in comments or group chats. Slowly, more people join in, posting hurtful messages, spreading rumors, or even editing the student’s photos to embarrass them. Sometimes, fake accounts are also created to target the person anonymously.



This usually happens step by step — it may start as a joke, but gradually turns into continuous harassment. The victim may receive messages, negative comments, or public posts that damage their confidence and reputation.



Students and teenagers are the most common targets because they use social media frequently and may feel pressured by peer opinions. The consequences can be serious, including stress, anxiety, loss of confidence, and in some cases, avoiding social interaction or studies.



To prevent cyberbullying, students should think before posting, avoid responding to harmful comments, block or report offenders, and talk to trusted people like teachers or parents. Creating a respectful and positive online environment is everyone’s responsibility.
